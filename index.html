Lung Cancer Study

Definition

Lung cancer is a malignant tumor characterized by uncontrolled cell growth in tissues of the lung.

Common Causes

  • Smoking: Primary cause (85% of cases).
  • Radon Gas: Radioactive exposure.
  • Air Pollution: Industrial chemicals.

Pathology Pictures

+ Gross Appearance

Large, irregular firm mass, usually grayish-white, often involving the hilum and invading the lung parenchyma.

+ Microscopic

Clusters of malignant epithelial cells. In Squamous types, look for Keratin Pearls and Intercellular Bridges.

Prevention

  • Quit Smoking: The most effective way to reduce risk.
  • Avoid Secondhand Smoke: Stay away from smoke-filled environments.
  • Test for Radon: Ensure your home/workplace is safe from radioactive gases.
  • Healthy Diet: High intake of fruits and vegetables may offer protection.
